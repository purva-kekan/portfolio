About Me

When analytics meet human insights

My journey into data analytics began with a simple belief: numbers should serve people, not the other way around. Over the past three years, I've had the privilege of working with organizations where data directly impacts human lives—from analyzing 1M+ child protective services records at KARA to help flag high-risk situations, to processing 65K+ public health service requests in Boston to ensure underserved communities receive the care they need. At IpserLab, I'm currently building analytics for an AI travel platform that prioritizes mental health and safety for vulnerable travelers, creating dashboards that monitor inclusivity and help reduce travel anxiety for women, LGBTQ+ individuals, and solo adventurers.

What drives me most is discovering the human story hidden within the data. Whether I'm using SARIMA forecasting to predict healthcare demand patterns, building Power BI dashboards with geospatial mapping to identify community health gaps, or developing predictive models with 90% accuracy to prevent medical supply shortages, I'm constantly asking: how can this insight make someone's day better, safer, or healthier? My work has improved targeted interventions by 25-40% across multiple organizations, but the real reward comes from knowing that behind every percentage point improvement, there are real people receiving better care, feeling safer, or finding the support they need when they need it most.
